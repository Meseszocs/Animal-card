Horse

photo credit: Ashley Kath-Bilsky

Horses' anatomy enables them to make use of speed to escape predators and they have a well-developed sense of balance and a strong fight-orflight response.

  • Scientific name: Equus ferus caballus
  • Lifespan: 25 to 30 years
  • Average height: 142 to 183 cm
  • Speed: The galop averages 40 to 80 km/h

Horses have evolved during the past 45-55 million years from small multi-toed creature to large single-toed animal. Domestication of horses started 4000 BC. Horses anatomy enables them to escape predators. According to studies horses showed developed intelligence in the areas of problem-solving, speed of learning and memory. Source: Wikipedia
